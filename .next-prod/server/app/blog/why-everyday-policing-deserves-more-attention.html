Why Everyday Policing Deserves More Attention

Public debate about policing tends to focus on dramatic moments: high-profile operations, scandals, and sweeping reform announcements.

Most policing, however, happens in routine encounters. A complaint filed at a station desk, a checkpoint conversation, a patrol through a familiar neighbourhood. These small moments shape how citizens experience the state far more than any headline.

Taking the everyday seriously changes the questions we ask. Instead of only asking whether a reform passed, we can ask how it lands in practice, who it reaches, and what informal routines grow around it.

Future posts will pick up specific examples from research in South Asian cities.
