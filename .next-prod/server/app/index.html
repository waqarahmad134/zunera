Associate Professor, University of Warwick

Policing, security & social order in the Global South

Zunera is an Associate Professor at the University of Warwick working on criminology, law and socio-legal studies. Her research focuses on law enforcement, digitalisation, cybercrime, surveillance and urban (in)security, with a particular emphasis on South Asia and the Global South. She studied at SOAS and King's College London, and held postdoctoral and teaching positions at UCL.

Featured book

Insecure Guardians: Enforcement, Encounters and Everyday Policing in Postcolonial Karachi

Hurst & Co. / Oxford University Press · 2022

Drawing on more than nineteen months of ethnographic fieldwork in Karachi, including interviews and participant observation inside police stations and training centres, this book examines how securitisation has shaped everyday policing in urban Pakistan. It traces how competing demands for security and stalled institutional reform have driven militarisation and a culture of procedural informality.
